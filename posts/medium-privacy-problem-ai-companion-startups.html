The Privacy Problem Every AI Companion Startup Has to Solve

AI companion products ask users to share more than ordinary app preferences. A chat can include mood, loneliness, fantasy, personal routines, relationship expectations, and private emotional context. That makes privacy a core product requirement, not a legal footer.

The first problem is consent. Users should understand what the app remembers, what it forgets, and which settings shape future conversations. If memory feels hidden, personalization can quickly become uncomfortable.

The second problem is control. A good companion app should make it easy to edit a profile, delete sensitive chat history, pause personalization, or start over. Those controls need to be visible before trust is lost, not buried after a support request.
